Places to see

Shibuya

Home of the Shibuya Crossing, the statue of famous dog Hachiko, and the hub of Japanese fashion. Shibuya is home to many high end street fashion retailers such as Supreme, BAPE, and Nike. With fashion being a highlight of this special ward, many people can be seen dressed to impressed. A great location to people watch anad see the latest trends in Japanese fashion for people of all ages.

Akihabara

Nicknamed the "Electric City," Akihabara is known as one of the centers of modern Japanese pop. It features a variety of retailers from video games, manga, and anime. Akhihabara is also home to Japan's largest collection of arcades from simple crane games, casual video games, to serious strategy games geared towards adult arcade goers. There are a variety of unique shops to experience such as maid cafes, full room multi-person VR studios, and board game shops to name a few.

Shinjuku

With vast selection of clubs, bars, and karaoke hotspots, you won't be able to explore the entirety of the city that never sleeps. The attractions in the area may be more suited for adults as the unique and interesting bars may only be access by people who are 20 years or older. From Robot bar shows, horror bars, and classic Japanese bars, Shinjuku has it all when it comes to adult beverages.

Unique Experiences

Teamlab Borderless Digital Art Museum

Price: $30. For all ages. Teamlab Borderless describes their museum as "a group of artworks without boundaries...that form one borderless world". The various digital artworks are dynamic, constantly in motion throuought the entire building. Artworks interact with one another differently, creatin a truely borderless world within their space, with every visit being an entirely different experience.

Ghibli Museum

Price: $10. For all ages. Fans of Studio Ghibli's films would be recommend to attend the Ghibli Museum where the studio's 3D artwork is on display. Giant 'My Neighbor Totoro' Statues and the cat bus daycare center are just some of things to look forward to at the museum. The daycay center not only takes care of your children but they will have an amazing time in the children's play place as you stroll through the beautiful Ghibli garden.

Sensoji Temple

Price: $10. For all ages. An ancient Buddhist temple. Tokyo's oldest and most significant temple with beautiful architecture. You will be welcomed by a huge lantern guarded by the deities Fujin-sama and Raigin-sama, the god of wind and the god of thunder and lightning. Many small shops line the road to the temple with similar architechture. The small shops themselves are a sight to see especially during tourist season, with so many people looking through the shops.
