IT Opportunities

The IT scene: Japan has bepone of the world's leaders in technology and houses a multitude of tech companies. Tokyo offers many opportunities in banking, game development, AI development and much more

SoftBank

Listed the 36th largest company in the world by the Forbes Global 2000 list, SoftBank is a multinational conglomerate holding company. Their primary focus is being and internet provider for the Japanese people, but are also involved in broadband, fixed telecoms, e-commerce, finance, media, and marketing. The wide scope of businesses and countries SoftBank is dealing with would be attractive for employee's seeking to work in many different situations. Learn more at their website.

Hitachi

Hitachi operates eleven business segments from Information & Telecommunication Systems to Construction Machinery. People all over the world recognize their electronic products from construction vehicles to consumer level cameras. Prospective employees with IT experience will be able to work within the companies main focus' in IT, energy, industry, transportation, medical equipment, and home appliances. Learn more at their website.

NTT Docomo

Docomo is the dominant mobile phone operator in Japan, providing phone and internet services. For the Japanese people, Docomo is the place to go to replace their older phone and to set up new phone plans at. Docomo is also partnered with T-Mobile in the US, allocating free cellular data for traveling T-Mobile customers. Docomo would be a great place to work for people interested in the field of telecommunications and internet access. Learn more at their website.

Nintendo

Nintendo is one the of world's largest videogame companies, with some of the most recognizable games series Mario, Legend of Zelda, and Pokemon. Nintendo has always been a giant in the gaming industry, and with their newest console, the Nintendo Switch, they have recently brought their brand to the forefront of family gaming once again. Fans of Nintendo's works would surely enjoy working for Nintendo themselves. Learn more at their website.
